## 5.1 FINANCIAL SYSTEM OVERVIEW

**Definition**: The Finance System governs monetary flows across the entire team operation. It integrates three income sources (sponsorship, prize money, TV rights distribution) with four expense categories (personnel, R&D, operations, facilities), creating a dynamic budget management challenge with seasonal cash flow variations.

### Financial Calendar

```
SEASON START (January)
  - Contract negotiation with sponsors (finalizes quarterly payments)
  - Budget allocation across departments
  - Prize money forecasts based on pre-season predictions
  - Loan availability assessment

Q1 (Races 1-6, 8 weeks)
  - Sponsorship Q1 payment received
  - First prize money distributions (after races 1-6)
  - Payroll: Monthly salaries paid
  - R&D spending accelerates (major projects begin)
  
Q2 (Races 7-12, 8 weeks)
  - Sponsorship Q2 payment received
  - Prize money from Races 7-12 distributed
  - Mid-season budget reviews (reforecasting)
  - Financial crisis possible (cost overruns, injuries, DNF streak)
  
Q3 (Races 13-18, 8 weeks)
  - Sponsorship Q3 payment received
  - Prize money distributions
  - Final push for championship (R&D investments peak)
  - Contingency funds near depletion
  
Q4 (Races 19-24, 8 weeks)
  - Sponsorship Q4 final payment received
  - Final prize money payments
  - Year-end accounting and audit
  - Off-season planning (budget for next year)
```

## 5.2 INCOME SOURCES

### 5.2.1 Sponsorship Contracts

**Sponsorship Structure**: 3-5 major sponsors + 5-10 minor sponsors

#### **MAJOR SPONSOR CONTRACT** (Typical)

```
Sponsor: TechCorp Energy (fictional)
Contract Length: 3 years (2027-2029)
Total Value: $30M/year

Payment Schedule:
  - Q1 (March): $7.5M
  - Q2 (June): $7.5M
  - Q3 (September): $7.5M
  - Q4 (December): $7.5M

Performance KPIs:
  1. Team Championship Position
     - Top-3 finish: Full sponsor payment ($30M)
     - 4-6 finish: 85% payment ($25.5M)
     - 7-10 finish: 60% payment ($18M)
     - Below 10: 40% payment ($12M, minimum)
  
  2. Podium Count
     - Bonus: +$500K per podium (up to 12 podiums = +$6M)
     - Malus: -$250K per podium missed vs. 5 podium target
  
  3. Win Count
     - Bonus: +$1M per win (up to 3 wins = +$3M)
     - If 0 wins: -$1.5M penalty
  
  4. Brand Visibility
     - Broadcast logo presence measured
     - Penalty if visibility <75% targeted level: -$1M
  
  5. Social Media Engagement
     - Team social posts mentioning sponsor: Minimum 20/season
     - Influencer posts: Minimum 10/season
     - Penalty if not met: -$500K

Year-End Calculation Example (Q4 Payment):
  - Base: $7.5M
  - Championship adjustment: Team finished 4th = 85% = $6.375M
  - Podium bonus: 8 podiums (target 5) = +$400K
  - Win bonus: 2 wins = +$2M
  - Brand visibility: 82% (above 75%) = 0 penalty
  - Social KPI: Met = 0 penalty
  - Q4 payment: $8.775M

Total Year 1: $30M + ~$2.5M bonuses - $1M malus = $31.5M actual
```

### 5.4.2 Cash Reserve Strategy

**Recommended Cash Reserve Levels:**

```
Minimum Safe Level: $5M
  - Covers 1 week of operations
  - Allows emergency hiring, medical costs
  - FIA requires proof of financial stability

Healthy Level: $10-15M
  - Covers 1+ month of operations
  - Buffer for revenue shortfalls
  - Flexibility for opportunities (mid-season acquisitions)

Strong Level: $20M+
  - Covers 1.5-2 months operations
  - Can absorb major crisis without borrowing
  - Typical for well-funded teams

Reserve Building Phases:
  - Off-season (December-February): Accumulate reserves
  - Early season (March-May): Maintain
  - Mid-season (June-August): Burn through (if needed for R&D)
  - Late season (September-November): Stabilize
  - Year-end (December): Rebuild
```

## 5.5 MID-SEASON FINANCIAL CRISIS

### 5.5.1 Crisis Scenarios

#### **SCENARIO A: Prize Money Shortfall**

```
Situation: Team expected to average 4th place finishes ($500K/race)
Actual: Team averaging 7th place finishes ($150K/race)
Impact per race: -$350K shortfall

Across 24 races (2 races of shortfall already):
  - Expected prize money: $12M (24 races × $500K)
  - Actual forecast: $6M (24 races × $250K)
  - Shortfall: -$6M

Financial Crisis Response:
  Option A) Cut R&D spending: -$3M (delay projects, hire freeze)
  Option B) Reduce driver bonuses: -$2M (morale hit, player #1 morale -20%)
  Option C) Take emergency loan: $6M at 10% interest (-$600K annual cost)
  Option D) Reduce staff: Fire 5 engineers (-$1.2M savings, -30% R&D speed)
  
  Likely solution: Combination of A + C
  - Cut R&D: -$2M
  - Emergency loan: $4M (at 10% = $400K/year cost)
  - Total shortfall covered: $6M
```

#### **SCENARIO B: Sponsor Contract Termination**

```
Situation: Major sponsor (FinanceBank, $20M/year) terminates mid-season
Reason: Team performance declining (now 7th, no podiums in 4 races)
Contract clause: Can terminate Q2 with 30-day notice

Impact:
  - Lost income: $20M/year
  - Q2 remaining: $5M (quarter payment already received)
  - Q3-Q4 lost: $15M

Financial Crisis:
  Quarterly budget now:
    Q2 (remaining 4 weeks): $5M incoming, $12M expenses = -$7M shortfall
    Q3: $0 sponsorship (TechCorp still paying), -$4M from other sponsors = -$4M net
    Q4: Recovery to $10M from remaining sponsors
    
  Total seasonal impact: -$25M income, $135M fixed expenses = IMPOSSIBLE BUDGET
  
  Survival Actions Required:
  1. Emergency cost reduction: -$15M (across all departments)
  2. Emergency loans: $10M (at 12% interest = $1.2M annual cost)
  3. Staff reduction: Fire 10 engineers (-$2.5M)
  4. Salary cuts: Reduce all staff 10% (-$4.5M)
  5. R&D freeze: No new projects, only bug fixes (-$8M)
  
  Result: Team becomes non-competitive, championship lost, potential bankruptcy risk
```

#### **SCENARIO C: Injury Crisis + Low Sponsorship**

```
Situation: Driver #1 injured (4-race absence), team championship position slides
Consequences:
  - Reserve driver salary: +$2M
  - Lost podiums: -4 expected podiums × $250K = -$1M bonus loss
  - Prize money shortfall: -$2M (expected 6 races top-3, actual mix)
  - Sponsor penalties: TechCorp drops from 85% to 60% = -$4.5M
  - Total impact: -$9.5M income + $2M new costs = -$11.5M net

Cash Position During Crisis:
  Pre-injury: $12M reserves
  Post-injury: $12M - $11.5M = $500K reserves (DANGEROUS)
  
  If second injury occurs: Team is BANKRUPT (cannot pay payroll)
  
  Mitigation (proactive):
  - Injury insurance: $2M/year covers salary, medical, contingencies
  - Cash buffer: Maintain $15M+ reserves
  - Sponsor stability: Focus on reliable long-term partners, not short-term deals
```

## 5.8 SPONSOR NEGOTIATION MECHANICS

### 5.8.1 Contract Renegotiation

**When Sponsor Requests Changes:**

```
Situation: Mid-season, FinanceBank (sponsor) reviews contract

FinanceBank Assessment (Week 15):
  - Expected performance: Top-3, 2-3 podiums by Race 12
  - Actual performance: 4th place, 1 podium
  - Sponsor satisfaction: DECLINING
  - Contract value: $20M (target year-end)
  - Potential payout: $17M (85% due to below-target performance)

Sponsor Options:
  A) Honor contract: Pay full $20M by season end
  B) Reduce payment: Reduce to $15M immediately, renegotiate
  C) Terminate: Exercise exit clause, cease payment (if contract allows)

Negotiation (Team Response):
  Scenario A: Accept reduced payment
    - Team receives: $15M instead of $20M
    - Impact: -$5M shortfall in Q4
    - Morale: Negative (shows sponsor dissatisfaction)
    - Recovery: Must improve performance to win back sponsor next year
  
  Scenario B: Propose Amendment
    - Team proposes: "Conditional bonus pool"
    - Structure: If top-3 by season end, sponsor pays additional $3M
    - Incentive: Motivates both team and sponsor
    - Cost: Gamble (if fail, lose $3M; if succeed, recover lost $5M)
  
  Scenario C: Counter-offer
    - Team proposes: Extended multi-year deal at reduced rate
    - Structure: $18M this year, guaranteed $17M for years 2-3
    - Benefit: Sponsor gets security, team gets guaranteed income
    - Risk: Locks in lower rate if team improves dramatically
```

## 5.10 BANKRUPTCY MECHANICS

**When Team Runs Out of Money:**

```
Condition: Team reserves reach $0, cannot meet payroll

Bankruptcy Timeline:

Week 1: Missed Payroll
  - Can't pay staff salaries
  - FIA investigation opens
  - Sponsors notified of financial distress
  - Staff departures begin (morale collapse)

Week 2: FIA Action
  - Formal financial compliance review
  - Demand proof of funds for rest of season
  - Potential race ban if cannot demonstrate solvency

Week 3: Administration
  - If team cannot secure emergency funding:
    - FIA-appointed administrator takes control
    - Team may be suspended from championship
    - Races forfeit, zero points awarded
    - Assets seized to pay creditors (facilities, equipment)

Week 4+: Restructuring/Liquidation
  - Team either:
    A) Sold to new owner (asset sale)
    B) Liquidated (wound up, dissolved)
    C) Acquired by larger parent company

Historical Note:
  - This is extremely rare (only happened 3 times in F1 history)
  - FIA actively prevents through financial controls
  - Teams have backstops (owner wealth, sponsor guarantees)
```
