# PART 2: GAME SYSTEMS
## SECTION 2.1: SEASON MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

---

## 2.1.1 SEASON OVERVIEW

**Definition**: The Season Management System governs the 24-race championship calendar, the progression of events, dramatic moments, and how a single season unfolds across 15-30 hours of gameplay.

### Season Structure

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Total Duration** | ~24 weeks in-game time (March - December, real F1 calendar) |
| **Number of Races** | 24 Grand Prix events |
| **Off-Season Windows** | 1-2 weeks between races (player management time) |
| **Total Gameplay Hours** | 15-30 hours per full season |
| **Playable Starts** | New career, continue career, specific season jump-in |

---

## 2.1.2 SEASON PHASES

### PHASE 1: PRE-SEASON (Weeks -2 to 0)
**Duration**: 2-3 hours gameplay  
**Objectives**: Prepare team for the championship

#### Player Actions:
1. **Driver Contracts** (Mandatory)
   - Confirm/negotiate contracts for 2 primary drivers
   - Hire 1 reserve/test driver
   - Set performance expectations and salary terms

2. **Base Preparation**
   - Conduct pre-season testing (improves driver familiarity, morale boost)
   - Review R&D progress from off-season
   - Allocate initial budget (pre-competition phase)

3. **Infrastructure Setup**
   - Confirm all facilities operational
   - Assign engineering teams to departments
   - Set season-long strategy priorities

4. **Target Setting**
   - Define season objectives (championship, points target, driver development)
   - Set sponsor KPIs (podium targets, manufacturer objectives)

#### Rewards/Consequences:
- ✅ Good preparation: +10% team morale, faster R&D ramp-up
- ❌ Poor preparation: -10% team morale, delayed development

---

### PHASE 2: CHAMPIONSHIP (Weeks 1-23)
**Duration**: 12-26 hours gameplay  
**Structure**: 24 races + off-track management

#### Race Weekend Cycle (repeats 24x)

**RACE WEEKEND STRUCTURE**: Friday-Sunday (or 2-day sprint format)

##### Friday: Practice Day(s)
- **Player Actions**:
  - Review track conditions and weather forecast
  - Analyze competitor pace (via telemetry/rivals' P1 times)
  - Plan tire strategies for Saturday/Sunday
  - Set up car setup philosophy (conservative vs aggressive)
  
- **Outcomes**:
  - Baseline lap times
  - Setup recommendations from engineers
  - Driver feedback on car balance

##### Saturday: Qualifying Day
- **Player Actions**:
  - Choose qualifying setup (qualifying-spec vs race-spec)
  - Plan tire strategy for qualifying
  - Receive real-time pit wall instructions during Q1/Q2/Q3
  - Potentially abort laps if damage/tire deg detected
  
- **Race Impact**: 
  - Grid position determined for Sunday race
  - Higher grid = better starting position, less tire deg in race 1
  - Affects tire strategy options in race

##### Sunday: Race Day
- **Race Duration**: 60-120 minutes (simulated)
- **Player Actions During Race**:
  - **Pit Strategy**: Decide pit stop timing, tire compound changes
  - **Fuel Management**: Monitor fuel consumption, adjust engine modes
  - **Driver Instructions**: Pit stop timing, track position targets, defend/attack
  - **Real-time Adjustments**: 
    - Weather changes (dry → rain → dry), requires strategy pivot
    - Competitor crashes (safety cars, VSC), opportunity for undercuts
    - Technical issues (vibration, DRS issues), inform driver, pit if needed
  - **Dynamic Conflict Resolution**: 
    - If driver disagrees with pit strategy ("I think we can do one more lap..."), negotiate
    - If team needs to override driver judgment, morale hit possible

- **Race Rewards**:
  - Points: 25-1 (1st-10th), fastest lap bonus +1
  - Prize money: $500K (10th) - $15M (1st)
  - Driver experience: varies by performance
  - Sponsor satisfaction: points target achievements
  - Engineer morale: successful strategies, clean races

---

#### OFF-SEASON BETWEEN RACES (1-2 weeks)

**Duration**: 2-4 hours gameplay per race cycle  
**Critical Phase**: Where Cycle B (Team Management) and Cycle C (R&D) dominate

##### A. TEAM MANAGEMENT ACTIONS

**Personnel Management**:
- Review driver morale (from race result)
- Check engineer morale (strategy quality, reliability issues)
- Address key staff departures (if any engineers leave)
- Plan driver development activities (sim racing, physical training)
- Conduct contract negotiations if underperformers need replacement

**Financial Adjustments**:
- Review race earnings vs. budget spending
- Allocate next race logistics budget
- Negotiate supplier payments (avoid penalties)
- Monitor sponsor contract compliance

**Crisis Management** (Emergent Events):
- Driver injured? → Activate reserve driver (2-4 race absence)
- Chief engineer poached? → Hire replacement, -20% R&D speed until re-staffed
- Major sponsor ultimatum? → Negotiate payment/retention or risk contract termination

##### B. R&D UPDATES & DEVELOPMENT

**New Component Arrivals**:
- Check if new aerodynamic packages ready for deployment
- Decide which components to use in next race (balance risk/reward)
- Monitor development timelines for major upgrades (target mid-season update at Race 12)

**Development Decisions**:
- Continue investing in existing programs (aero, power unit, reliability)
- Pivot focus based on competitiveness (if losing, aggressive new tech path)
- Plan for token allocation (8-10 tokens per season, manage carefully)

##### C. NEWS & DRAMATIC EVENTS

**Randomly Generated / Triggered Events**:
- ✅ "New talent scout report: Junior driver XXX had breakout performance in F2. Worth monitoring for future seasons."
- ⚠️ "Aerodynamic tunnel malfunction detected in data. Lost 1 week of development time."
- ❌ "Engine development hit unexpected heat dissipation problem. Need $2M additional budget to fix."
- 🤝 "Rival team's chief aerodynamicist left for Mercedes. Sources suggest leaked your advanced wing design."
- 💰 "Sponsor ultimatum: Minimum 5 podiums this season, or lose $5M contract."
- 🏆 "Your young driver finished 3rd in a key circuit. Media speculation about his future grows."
- ⚡ "Regulatory change announced: New aero restrictions effective Race 15. All teams must adapt."

**Consequences**:
- Events trigger decisions that cascade into future races
- High-risk choices (aggressive tech) lead to dramatic failures or breakthroughs
- Character-driven narratives emerge (driver rivalries, engineer heroics, sponsor drama)

### PHASE 3: MID-SEASON INFLECTION POINT (Race 12)
**Critical Juncture**: Mid-season regulation adjustments and major technical updates

#### Regulatory Balance of Performance
- **Purpose**: Prevent dominant team from running away with championship
- **Mechanics**:
  - If one team leads championship by 60+ points, they get penalty
  - Penalties vary: fuel capacity reduction, DRS adjustment, wind tunnel time cut
  - Historical precedent: F1 mid-season technical directives

#### Major Technical Updates
- **Constructors' Token Reset**: Each team gets refresh of development tokens (was limiting progress since Race 1)
- **New Component Waves**: 2-3 major new aerodynamic packages possible before this deadline
- **Power Unit Enhancements**: New engine spec (within homologation rules) can be deployed

#### Strategic Implication
- **Comeback Opportunity**: Trailing teams can invest heavily in new technology
- **Consolidation Path**: Leading teams decide: safe incremental updates or risky performance jump

---

### PHASE 4: END-OF-SEASON SPRINT (Races 21-24)
**Final 4 Races**: High-stakes, limited development capability

#### Resource Constraints
- **Development Tokens Depleted**: No more new major updates possible
- **Engine Mileage Limits**: Power units near end-of-life (more reliability risk)
- **Financial Pressure**: Prize money crucial for next season funding

#### Strategic Focus
- **Tire Strategy Mastery**: With no new hardware, pit strategy becomes everything
- **Driver Performance**: Raw driving skill now dominates (setup changes minimal)
- **Reliability Gambling**: Risk pushing engines harder for qualification speed vs. DNF in race

#### Championship Pressure
- **"Winner Takes All" Mentality**: Last races often decisive
- **Tactical Blocking**: Teams use #2 drivers to block rivals' overtakes
- **Safety Car Lottery**: Unpredictable safety cars can reset race dynamics

## 2.1.3 CHAMPIONSHIP SCORING & PROGRESSION

### Points System
- **1st Place**: 25 points
- **2nd Place**: 18 points
- **3rd Place**: 15 points
- **4th Place**: 12 points
- **5th Place**: 10 points
- **6th Place**: 8 points
- **7th Place**: 6 points
- **8th Place**: 4 points
- **9th Place**: 2 points
- **10th Place**: 1 point
- **Fastest Lap Bonus**: +1 point (only if finish in top 10)
- **DNF (Did Not Finish)**: 0 points

### Dual Championships
**Drivers' Championship**: Individual driver points accumulate  
**Constructors' Championship**: Team points (both drivers' points combined)

### Tie-Breaking Rules
- Head-to-head results (H2H record)
- Fastest lap count
- Most 1st place finishes
- Wins in latter races weighted heavier

## 2.1.4 EVENT GENERATION & DRAMA SYSTEM

### Event Architecture
Events are **procedurally generated** based on three factors:

#### Factor 1: PLAYER DECISIONS (Deterministic)
- **Your Actions Trigger Events**
  - Pushed R&D aggressively → Can succeed (+15% performance) OR fail (wasted 3 races of development)
  - Hired unproven young driver → Breakout star OR consistent underperformer
  - Skipped maintenance budget → Reliability issues later in season
  - Aggressive pit strategy → Undercut success OR strategic failure

#### Factor 2: RIVAL AI BEHAVIOR (Deterministic + Random)
- **AI Teams Create Ripple Effects**
  - Rival team's driver crashes out → Safety car, reshuffles entire race strategy
  - Competitor develops major breakthrough → You fall behind, must adapt
  - Rival team's sponsor demands met → They get funding advantage
  - Rival engineer leaves for another team → Leaks your secrets OR strengthens competitor

#### Factor 3: ENVIRONMENTAL / RANDOM (Stochastic)
- **Base Probability Events** (happen regardless of your decisions, but frequency varies)
  - Driver injury (2-5% per race)
  - Engine failure (1-3% depending on reliability focus)
  - Aerodynamic tunnel malfunction (1% per season)
  - Sponsor ultimatum (2-3% per season)
  - Regulatory changes (mid-season, deterministic)

### Event Probability Modifiers

| Event Type | Base Probability | Increased By | Decreased By |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|------------|
| **Driver Injury** | 3%/race | Aggressive race tactics, poor driver morale | Sim training, good morale |
| **Reliability Failure** | 2%/race | Aggressive R&D, poor maintenance budget | Conservative specs, high maintenance |
| **Aerodynamic Breakthrough** | 5%/season | High R&D budget, experienced engineers | Low budget, junior staff |
| **Sponsor Demands** | 10%/season | Poor recent results, low morale | Strong results, high sponsor satisfaction |
| **Key Staff Departure** | 8%/season | Low salary, poor working conditions, job offers from rivals | Competitive salary, good morale, career growth |
| **Tech Leak** | 3%/season | Rival espionage rolls, low security protocol | High team morale, stable staff |

## 2.1.5 EXAMPLE SEASON NARRATIVES

### Narrative Arc 1: "The Comeback"
**Setup**: Start 5th in constructors' standings, slow early season  
**Event Trigger**: Race 6 — Aerodynamic tunnel breakthrough generates major new wing design  
**Development**: Aggressive R&D investment pays off, new package debuts Race 8  
**Climax**: Mid-season, you're now P2, catch-up race with leader  
**Resolution**: Final races are intense competitive battle, you win or lose championship in final race

### Narrative Arc 2: "The Collapse"
**Setup**: Start as championship favorite with strong car  
**Event Trigger**: Race 9 — Chief engineer poached by rival team, takes designs with them  
**Development**: Development stalls, rivals catch up  
**Climax**: By Race 18, you've lost championship lead  
**Resolution**: Either recover with emergency upgrades or finish 2nd

### Narrative Arc 3: "The Dark Horse"
**Setup**: Start as mid-field team with young, hungry drivers  
**Event Trigger**: Race 3 — Reserve driver gets surprise podium, generates hype  
**Development**: Small budget stretched creatively, careful tactical choices  
**Climax**: Underdog story, team climbs standings consistently  
**Resolution**: Surprise podium finish or breakthrough win

## 2.1.9 REPLAYABILITY MECHANISMS

**Why Each Season is Different**:

1. **Procedural Event Generation**: Different events trigger based on your decisions
2. **AI Team Variation**: Rival teams take different paths (aggressive vs conservative)
3. **Random Driver Talent Pool**: Each season spawns different junior driver talent
4. **Regulatory Mutations**: Mid-season technical directives vary
5. **Weather Patterns**: Race conditions random within realistic parameters
6. **Personality Conflicts**: Driver/engineer conflicts emerge dynamically

**Result**: No two seasons play identically. High replay value.
