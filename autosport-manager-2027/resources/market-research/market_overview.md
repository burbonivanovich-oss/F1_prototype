### Direct Competitor #1: F1 Manager 2024

**Strengths**:
- Official F1 licensing (teams, drivers, tracks)
- Massive player base (600K+ sales)
- Deep team management systems
- Great mobile version
- Yearly release cadence

**Weaknesses**:

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### HIGH-PRIORITY RISKS

#### Risk #1: Licensing Requirements
**Impact**: Critical
**Probability**: High (70%)
**Mitigation**:
- Launch with **generic/fictional teams initially** (not a blocker)
- Plan licensing partnerships for V1.1 or later
- Budget $200K-500K for licensing deals
- Consider smaller series (WEC, Formula E cheaper than F1)

**Recommendation**: Ship with generic teams. Licensing becomes update feature.

---

#### Risk #2: AI Complexity (Emergent Behavior)
**Impact**: High
**Probability**: Medium (50%)
**Mitigation**:
- Start with rule-based AI (simpler, more predictable)
- Progress to machine learning-based decisions (post-launch)
- Robust playtesting for balance
- Community feedback integration

**Recommendation**: Rule-based AI for V1.0. ML AI for V1.2+.

---

#### Risk #3: Performance at Scale
**Impact**: Medium
**Probability**: Medium (45%)
**Mitigation**:
- Target 20-team grids initially (common)
- Optimize simulation for multi-season runs
- Profile heavily early in development
- Consider server-side simulation (future version)

**Recommendation**: Solid architecture from day 1. Optimization budgeted in production.

---

#### Risk #4: Platform-Specific Issues
**Impact**: Medium
**Probability**: Low (25%)
**Mitigation**:
- Extensive Windows/Steam testing
- Budget for Proton/Linux support
- Early Deck certification (if desired)

**Recommendation**: Steam is the only V1.0 platform. Multi-platform for V1.5+.
