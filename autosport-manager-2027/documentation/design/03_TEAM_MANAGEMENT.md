## 3.1 TEAM MANAGEMENT OVERVIEW

**Definition**: The Team Management System controls all aspects of hiring, contracting, developing, and maintaining personnel. It directly impacts R&D speed, race performance, morale, and financial health.

### Core Principles
- **Personnel = Performance Multiplier**: Quality staff directly boosts team capabilities
- **Morale = Velocity**: Happy engineers work faster; sad engineers leave or work slower
- **Contracts = Long-term Planning**: 2-3 year commitments require strategic foresight
- **Specialization = Expertise**: Engineers have individual specialties (aero, powertrain, reliability)
- **Conflict Resolution = Drama**: Personality clashes create emergent storytelling

## 3.3 ENGINEERS: HIRING & SPECIALIZATION

### Engineer Pool & Recruitment

**Available Engineers**: ~150 engineers in the global F1 talent pool at any time

#### Engineer Categories by Experience Level

| Level | Description | Salary Range | Performance | Availability |
|-------|-------------|--------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Junior** | 0-2 years experience, newly hired | $200K-400K | Baseline (1.0x) | 30-40 available |
| **Mid-Level** | 3-5 years, some championship experience | $600K-1.2M | +20% specialist bonus | 20-30 available |
| **Senior** | 6-10 years, multiple teams, proven leaders | $1.5M-3M | +50% specialist bonus | 10-15 available |
| **World-Class** | 10+ years, championship winners, ex-Ferrari/Mercedes | $3M-8M | +100% specialist bonus | 2-5 available (high turnover) |

### Engineer Specializations

Each engineer has **one primary specialization**:

| Specialty | Role | Impact Area | Unique Contribution |
|-----------|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Aerodynamics** | CFD, wind tunnel, aero setup | Vehicle pace | +0.2-0.5 sec/lap per upgrade |
| **Powertrain** | Engine tuning, ERS management, power delivery | Top speed + acceleration | +5-8% power output |
| **Suspension/Chassis** | Setup optimization, handling, reliability | Cornering speed | +0.1-0.3 sec in medium/slow corners |
| **Reliability** | Durability, wear prediction, failure analysis | DNF prevention | -30% failure rate per specialist |
| **Strategy** | Pit wall decision support, tire/fuel analysis | Race strategy quality | +2-3 positions avg in strategic races |
| **Data Analysis** | Telemetry interpretation, opponent analysis | Information advantage | Unlock competitor strategies, setup insights |

### Hiring Mechanisms

#### Method 1: Open Market Hiring (Always Available)
- **Frequency**: Anytime, anytime off-season or between races
- **Pool**: 10-15 engineers available each week
- **Process**:
  1. View available engineers with stats (experience, specialty, salary expectation)
  2. Negotiate contract (2-3 years)
  3. Immediate hire (1 week integration time)
- **Cost**: Salary + $500K-2M signing bonus (varies by desirability)

#### Method 2: Competitive Poaching (Mid-Season)
- **Frequency**: 1-3 times per season, rival teams lose key staff
- **Scenario**: "Mercedes' Head of Aerodynamics considering offers. Salary expectation: $4.5M/year"
- **Competition**: Other teams bid simultaneously
- **Win Condition**: Offer highest salary + promise of advancement
- **Risk**: If you poach someone, rivals may poach yours back (retaliation)
- **Reward**: Gain world-class engineer mid-season, rival team weakened

#### Method 3: Junior Development Program (Long-term)
- **Frequency**: Ongoing, can hire 1-2 juniors per season
- **Timeline**: Takes 2-3 seasons to develop junior → mid-level engineer
- **Benefit**: Cheap ($300K), loyal (raised in your org), familiar with your systems
- **Risk**: High failure rate (30-40% don't develop into useful engineers)
- **Reward**: Cheap world-class engineer if successful

**Standard Contract (2-3 years)**:

```

Signing Bonus: $1M
Release Clause: Can be released after Year 1 for $3M penalty

```

### Key Contract Terms

| Term | Options | Impact |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Length** | 1 / 2 / 3 years | Longer = stability, shorter = flexibility |
| **Base Salary** | $200K - $8M | Higher = happier engineer, higher morale |
| **Performance Bonus** | 0-20% of base | Incentivizes success, can be championship-based |
| **Release Clause** | Yes/No | If Yes, can exit early (costs money) |
| **Non-Compete** | Yes/No | If Yes, engineer can't join rival for 1 year post-contract |
| **Development Budget** | $0-500K/year | Training, tools, education (boosts skill growth) |

### Morale & Loyalty System

**Morale Range**: 0-100%

#### Factors Affecting Morale (Weekly Updates)

| Factor | Impact | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Race Results** | +15% for podium finish, -20% for DNF | Single race |
| **Successful R&D Delivery** | +10% when new part deployed successfully | One-time |
| **Failed Development** | -15% when part fails/performs poorly | Lasting (3-4 weeks) |
| **Salary Payment** | +5% for on-time, -25% if delayed | One-time |
| **Promotion** | +20% when promoted to lead role | Lasting (full contract) |
| **Demotion** | -30% when demoted or sidelined | Lasting (until reassigned) |
| **Team Morale Event** | ±5% from external events (turmoil, good news) | One-time |
| **Overwork** | -5% per week if assigned to 3+ major projects simultaneously | Accumulates |
| **Management Conflict** | -10% if player makes unpopular decision | Lasting (2-3 weeks) |
| **External Job Offer** | -20% if competing team actively recruiting | Lasting (until resolved) |

#### Morale Thresholds & Consequences

| Morale Level | R&D Speed | Staff Risk | Performance | Behavior |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **80-100%** | +25% faster | 0% turnover risk | Max capability | Proactive, offers ideas |
| **60-79%** | +10% faster | 5% turnover risk | Normal | Competent, reliable |
| **40-59%** | Baseline (1.0x) | 15% turnover risk | Slightly reduced | Disengaged, clock-watching |
| **20-39%** | -20% slower | 40% turnover risk | Poor | Considering departure |
| **0-19%** | -50% slower | 80% turnover risk | Critical failure | Will definitely leave next month |

#### Turnover (Staff Departure)

**Turnover Triggers**:
1. **Low Morale Departure** (Automatic): If morale <20% for 4+ weeks, engineer leaves
2. **Rival Poaching** (Random): Weekly 5-10% chance a rival team makes offer
3. **Retirement** (Time-based): After 15+ years, engineer considers retirement
4. **Conflict Resolution** (Player Choice): Fire unpopular engineer or accept department exodus

**Turnover Consequences**:
- **Knowledge Loss**: Departing engineer takes 10-20% of accumulated R&D in their specialty
- **Morale Hit**: Remaining team loses 10-15% morale if close colleague leaves
- **Delay**: Replacement takes 1-2 weeks to integrate and reach full productivity

### Driver Development System

**Driver Skill Curve**:
- Drivers improve 2-5% per season naturally (experience gain)
- Additional development via:
  - **Simulator Training**: +2% per season with dedicated training budget
  - **Coaching**: +3% if paired with strong driver coach
  - **Successful Results**: +1-2% per season with regular podiums
  - **Mentorship**: +2% if paired with veteran teammate (peer effect)

**Driver Attributes** (0-100 scale):

| Attribute | Definition | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Pace** | Raw speed, qualifying performance | Lap time: -0.5 to +0.5 sec difference |
| **Racecraft** | Overtaking, defensive driving, racecraft | Position gains: ±2-3 positions |
| **Consistency** | Reliability, mistake avoidance | DNF rate variation |
| **Adaptability** | Speed to learn new setups, tire management | Setup change effectiveness |
| **Mental Toughness** | Pressure handling, comebacks | Performance under pressure |
| **Youth Factor** | Natural progression room | Potential ceiling |

### Driver Interactions & Conflicts

#### Teammate Dynamics

**Conflict Types**:
1. **Performance Gap**: If one driver significantly outperforms, loser may request trade
2. **Personality Clash**: Incompatible drivers can request different teams
3. **Team Favoritism**: If player heavily favors P1, P2 morale drops
4. **Public Rivalry**: Media amplifies tensions, sponsor pressure increases

**Conflict Resolution Options**:
- **Separate Efforts**: Treat equally, no favorites
- **Clear Hierarchy**: P1 prioritized (risky, P2 may leave)
- **Mentorship Model**: P1 develops P2 (requires compatible personalities)
- **Trade One Out**: Sell underperforming driver (financial loss, morale boost)

### Department Efficiency Multipliers

**Base R&D Speed Formula**:
```
R&D_Speed = Base_Time × (1 + Engineer_Skill) × (1 + Facility_Bonus) × (1 + Morale_Multiplier) × (1 + CTO_Bonus)
```

**Example Calculation**:
```
New Aero Package Development Time = 4 weeks
- Base Time: 4 weeks
- Engineer Skill Bonus: Senior Aerodynamicist (+50%) = 4 × 0.5 = 2 weeks saved
- Facility Bonus: Level 4 Wind Tunnel (+40%) = 2 × 0.4 = 0.8 weeks saved
- Morale Multiplier: Team at 85% morale (+25%) = 1.2 weeks saved
- CTO Leadership: +15% bonus = 0.3 weeks saved

Final Time = 4 - 2 - 0.8 - 1.2 - 0.3 = **-0.3 weeks** (actually faster than baseline!)
```

## 3.7 PERSONNEL EVENTS & DRAMA

### Procedurally Generated Personnel Events

#### Event Type 1: Rival Poaching Offers

**Trigger**: Random weekly (5-10% chance), or guaranteed if your star engineer in demand

**Example**:
> "McLaren has made an offer to your Chief Aerodynamicist James Richardson:
> - Salary: $4.5M/year (vs your current $2.4M)
> - Position: Chief Technical Officer role
> - Offer expires in 1 week
> 
> **Your Options**:
> 1. Match salary ($4.5M) and promote to CTO
> 2. Counteroffer with lower salary, hope he stays
> 3. Let him leave (loss of key expertise)
> 4. Negotiate release clause if he's contractually locked"

#### Event Type 2: Personality Conflicts

**Trigger**: When mismatched drivers or incompatible staff paired

**Example**:
> "Your two drivers, Anderson and Verstappen-clone, had heated argument in team debrief.
> Anderson claims favoritism toward Verstappen.
> Team morale -15%.
> 
> **Your Options**:
> 1. Team meeting to clarify equal treatment (+5% morale if successful)
> 2. Separate their schedules and briefings (quick fix, doesn't solve root)
> 3. Trade Anderson to another team (lose driver, morale boost)
> 4. Trade Verstappen-clone (lose talent, gain harmony)"

#### Event Type 3: Unexpected Breakthrough

**Trigger**: High R&D investment + experienced engineer + favorable RNG (5-10% per season)

**Example**:
> "Your suspension engineer just had breakthrough idea!
> 
> 'Double-element damping system' could improve handling +0.2 sec.
> Development time: 3 weeks
> Risk: 40% chance of failure (wasted time)
> 
> **Proceed with development?**
> 1. Go aggressive (full development, 40% risk)
> 2. Conservative approach (slower, 10% risk)
> 3. Shelve idea (no benefit, no cost)"

#### Event Type 4: Driver Injury

**Trigger**: Random race result mechanic (3-5% per race) or crash-related

**Example**:
> "Your primary driver had heavy crash in Race 5.
> Medical evaluation: 3-race absence (possible return Race 8).
> 
> **Action Required**:
> - Activate reserve driver (automatic)
> - Cancel his medical sponsorships during absence (-$1M)
> - Reserve driver capability: 70% of primary driver performance
> 
> Estimated performance loss: 6-8 points over 3 races"

## 3.8 TEAM MORALE & DYNAMICS

### Overall Team Morale (Organizational Level)

**Calculation**:
```
Team_Morale = (Engineer_Morale_Avg × 0.4) + (Driver_Morale × 0.3) + (Recent_Results × 0.3)
```

### Morale Thresholds (Team Level)

| Morale | Status | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **80-100%** | Euphoric | +15% R&D speed, no turnover, media loves team |
| **60-79%** | Content | +5% R&D speed, minimal turnover, stable |
| **40-59%** | Neutral | Baseline performance, minor turnover (3-5%/month) |
| **20-39%** | Troubled | -10% R&D speed, high turnover (15%/month) |
| **0-19%** | Crisis | -30% R&D speed, mass exodus, sponsor withdrawal |

### Morale Recovery Options

**Quick Fixes** (1-2 weeks impact):
- Team celebration event ($500K cost, +10% morale temporary)
- Bonus payout ($2M, +15% morale temporary)
- Public statement of confidence (+5% morale)

**Medium-term** (4-8 weeks):
- Promote undervalued engineer (+10% morale for team, +30% for individual)
- Bring in external motivational speaker (+8% morale)
- Organizational restructuring (+20% morale if successful, -10% if fails)

**Long-term** (Full season):
- Winning races (steady +2% per podium)
- Championship success (sustained +20-30% morale boost)
- New facility upgrades (long-term culture shift)

## 3.9 MANAGER AI (Team Operations)

**Team Manager AI** handles administrative tasks automatically:

- **Payroll**: Ensures salaries paid on time (prevents morale penalties)
- **Scheduling**: Manages engineer workloads (prevents overwork penalties)
- **Conflict Mediation**: Attempts to resolve small conflicts (success varies)
- **Reporting**: Weekly morale reports, turnover alerts
- **Recommendations**: Suggests hiring/firing based on performance analysis

**Player Override Authority**: You can override AI decisions, but may incur morale penalties if unpopular
